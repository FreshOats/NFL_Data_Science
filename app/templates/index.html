This project is an investigation of concussions and the biomechanics involved in plays that have led to concussions in the NFL.
The purpose is to investigate high risk conditions that predictors for concussion injuries to help identify players who may have suffered even a subconcussive injury.
The identification of high risk plays can be applied not only to football, but any sport that allows for tracking the players movement in time, helping coaches and the players themselves seek medical attention when there aren't obvious signs of a concussion.
Prevening these high risk situations can be done by manipulating the movement of players to reduce the torque and impulse subjected to the players' heads in high-impact plays.
An additional outcome of this study can help identify higher risk players in the NFL, as players that sustain an injury one week that continue to play, are more likely to get seriously injured shortly after, without ample recovery time.
